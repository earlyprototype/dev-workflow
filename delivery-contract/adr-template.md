## Context
What forced the decision. The problem, the constraints, and the facts that were
true at the time — enough that someone who wasn't there understands the pressure
without needing the meeting. Name the alternatives you actually weighed, not just

## Decision
What was decided, in the active voice — "We do X." Specific enough that a reader
can tell whether a later change contradicts it. If the decision has a mechanical
enforcement (a hook, a required check, a branch ruleset), name it here, so the
enforcement and the reasoning live together.

## Consequences
What follows — the good and the costs. What becomes easier, what becomes harder,
what new obligation this creates. State the limits plainly: a known gap named
here is not a failure, it is honest scope.

**Revisit if:** the specific condition that would reopen this — a new
requirement, a tool that closes a gap named above, a real case the decision
didn't anticipate. A decision with no revisit trigger is one you can't safely
change later, because nobody will know it's safe to.
